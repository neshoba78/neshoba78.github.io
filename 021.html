Communicating Emergent Futures - Three Learning Objectives

February 5, 2021

At the end of this class the student will be able to:

-use existing platforms to make and communicate "meaning"

-focus on multi-scalar relationships--beyond MDEF--within their mapped ecosystem: local community, broader community of practice, and the world

-understand how interventions embed ideas, opinions, and attitudes through storytelling to shape emergent futures

Initial Questions

What is the definition of "meaning" in this course?

How do interventions embody ideas, opinions and attitudes?

Facts: An objective account of what happened

We were asked to identify three learning objectives. I decided to reword the course description into a bulleted list. There were some basic threads that we explored earlier in the program such as the idea of a multi scalar ecosystem which designers must map and navigate. I further decided to use the structure from Santi to reflect more deeply.

Feelings: The emotional reactions to the situation

With my background as a singer and legend keeper for my Tribe, I have a sense of how stories work in our own context. Stories can be one sentence or one paragraph. What happens is that as the storyteller recounts the piece, new meanings emerge. Stories are a journey in this way. There's deeper levels of knowledge, but it takes time to access them.

Findings: The concrete learning that you can take away from the situation

By setting my own learning objectives, I approach the class in a way of learning by teaching. Furthermore, the learning objectives are an intervention in themselves as it is a collaborative design through feedback on a short description of a course. I don't know much about the structure or the content, but I can set goals based on bits of information.

Future: Structuring your learning such that you can use it in the future

What will be interesting to see is whether the learning objectives will be allowed to be adjusted based upon the content as the course progresses.

Final Open Questions

Will the objectives be adjusted?

What meaning will the objectives take on as the course progresses?

How will I self-assess my work in this course?
