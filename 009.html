Atlas of Weak Signals Session 2 - Surveillance Capitalism

In thinking about the Atlas of Weak Signals, I decided to explore in terms of Indigenous Futurism themes as curated in the collection walking the clouds which is edited by Grace Dillon. Starting in November 2019, I facilitated a reading group titled “indigenous futurism 2420: 400 years / 5 moons.” The reading group met on each new and full moon and read through the collection while reflecting on each of the stories. We did this within the Boston Makers makerspace.

Thinking about Attention Protection in terms of:
Contact - reframing resistance and oppression in a way that implicates the part of individuals in larger realities.

Thinking about Dismantling Filter Bubbles in terms of:
Native Slipstream - non-linear thinking about space-time. navigating pasts, presents, and futures in one stream.

Thinking about Circular Data Economy in terms of:
Indigenous Science and Sustainability - traditional sustainable practices constitute a science, despite a lack of an analogous taxonomy to western science.

Thinking about The Truth Wars in terms of:
Native Apocalypse - transition from a state of imbalance to a state of balance by illustrating trauma to provide healing.

Thinking about Redesigning Social in terms of:
Biskaabiiyang, "Returning to Ourselves" - investigating how one is affected by colonization, discarding emotional and psychological baggage, and adapting to a post-Native Apocalypse world.
