Living with your own Ideas

November 6, 2020

Magic Machines

Embodied & First Person Perspective in Speculative Design

Q: How can you take action on your concept / topic area of interest in a brave way?

Part of my area of interest is in Indigenous survivance which is defined by Gerald Vizenor in Manifest Memoirs as, "Survivance is an active sense of presence, the continuance of native stories, not a mere reaction, or a survivable name. Native survivance stories are renunciations of dominance, tragedy and victimry". I want to embody a future point in time in which Indigenous knowledge held in a specific space needs to be curated. This would be a point beyond a certain Native Apocalypse. Perhaps even a situation such as salvaging knowledge after a tragic event, such as the fire at the National Museum of Brazil. What would it mean to recover data in such an event with a limited tool set?

Q: How can you continue to probe the design space of your topic through an embodied / first person exploration?

As I am conducting my work remotely in a Design Studio space in Boston, I decided to go to a place where I had access to a wealth of resources. The former executive director's office in the North American Indian Center of Boston holds an extensive collection of artifacts and texts. It is a prospective museum and library space. Without the funding to conduct the project, the resources sit in the office without any current use.

Q: Can you create a situation that might reveal something to you?

Building on the idea of the magic machines, I decided to go with as limited of a toolset as possible. I took my Canon Rebel T5 DSLR camera and laptop. I also made a conscious decision to limit the digital tool set. I decided to author a csv file in Terminal. I used LibreOffice only once for formatting. I also chose not to research archiving methods online and rely on my own instincts for what was the critical metadata to capture. Finally, it was important to form a personal connection with the collection. I received permission to isolate myself and stay overnight in the office to better understand the resources.

Q: How can you feel emotions surrounding your topic?

I initially felt overwhelmed by the volume of resources in the office. It was difficult to maneuver in the space without intentional organization. Overtime, I chose to hold waste such as empty boxes and packing material in the room until the experience was concluded. These items were just as part of the archive as the resources with vital data.

Q: Is there a small part of your topic that you could isolate and explore?

After exploring the space and the resources, I found evidence of adoption of digital design and electronics production as early as the 1980's. While my topic is envisioning futures and applications of digital technologies for interventions, I found it was important to recognize the history of the community as a framework to guide consultations on priorities and needs.
